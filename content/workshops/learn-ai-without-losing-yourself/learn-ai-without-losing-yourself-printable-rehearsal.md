# Learn AI Without Losing Yourself — Printable rehearsal script

> Facilitator copy. Timings assume a **60-minute** room; compress beats proportionally for 45 or 30.

## Opening (0:00–6:00)

Welcome. Thank you for being here. This is **not** a certification, a vendor hour, or a dare to “keep up.” It is a structured pause: how to use generative tools **without** outsourcing your judgment, your credit lines, or your voice.

Housekeeping: restrooms, Wi‑Fi, chat if virtual, and a promise—I will name limits honestly. Models change weekly; patterns of authorship change slowly. We are here for the slow part.

**Optional line (lighter):** If your group is tense, add: “If the Wi‑Fi dies, we still have judgment. If the model hallucinates, we still have judgment. Judgment is the app that never logs you out.”

### Opening through-line (read slowly)

You are not behind. You are busy. The culture around AI sells **velocity**; your work sells **meaning**. Today we will not chase features. We will practice four moves—**Pressure, Prompt, Problem, Practice**—then walk two scenarios you can steal for your own contexts.

Close the opening with a single sentence participants can repeat:

> “Voice is a practice, not a file format.”

---

## Pressure (6:00–12:00)

**Screen:** blank slide or a simple list title: “Pressures (not facts).”

Invite shout-outs or sticky notes: *What did the room feel this month about AI?* Cluster: speed, comparison, admin fear, grant language, student confusion, board asks.

**Teaching goal:** Externalize shame. Pressure is **data**, not destiny.

**Debrief line:** “We are not solving your institution in one hour. We are naming weather so you can dress for it.”

**Optional edutainment:** Read a breathless headline in a deadpan voice, then: “That was marketing. Here is the pattern underneath…”

---

## Prompt (12:00–22:00)

**Screen:** side-by-side “vague prompt” vs “bounded prompt.”

Walk a live rewrite. Minimum viable structure:

1. **Audience** — who reads this?
2. **Job** — what should change in the world after they read it?
3. **Constraints** — length, tone, taboo topics, citation rules.
4. **Evaluation** — how will *you* know it worked?

**Teaching goal:** Constraints beat cleverness.

**Critical takeaway:** If you cannot describe success, the model will confidently improvise.

---

## Problem (22:00–30:00)

**Screen:** one line: “What job are we hiring the model for?”

Pairs (2–3 minutes): write **one sentence**. Rotate: listener repeats the sentence verbatim.

**Teaching goal:** Problem before tool.

**Bridge:** “If the problem statement moved while you talked, that is good—that means you learned. Reset the prompt.”

---

## Practice (30:00–42:00)

**Screen:** critique tags: `voice · fact · consent · credit · audience · scope`

Short generated paragraph on screen (prepared). Timer **4 minutes**. Pairs pick **two tags** and produce two concrete edits—not vibes.

**Teaching goal:** Structured critique beats taste wars.

**Close beat:** “Document one prompt that worked. Models forget; your notes should not.”

---

## Scenarios (42:00–52:00)

Offer a menu—participants vote:

1. **Grant paragraph** (claims + ethics language)
2. **Syllabus / policy email** (norms + boundaries)
3. **Studio crit** (intent vs tool talk)

Run one table read. Facilitator keeps time and **tags** responses.

**Teaching goal:** Transfer.

**Line to land:** “Separate tool talk from intent talk.”

---

## Closing (52:00–60:00)

**Screen:** formats (30 / 45 / 60 / custom) + contact.

Repeat the four-move sequence once more, slowly.

**Best next step (homework):** One loop documented this week—prompt, output, two critique tags, revised line.

**Optional appendix — edutainment**

- **Worst prompt contest** (30 seconds): collect a hilariously vague prompt, fix it aloud.
- **One meme-level analogy per segment max**—then name the pattern immediately.

---

## End of script

Thank the hosts. Invite institutional follow-up via the workshop inquiry email. Remind participants where curriculum and lab notes live on the portal.
